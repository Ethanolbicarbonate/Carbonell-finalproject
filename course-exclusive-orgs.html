Our Student Organizations:
Fostering Community and Growth

Be a Part of Our Community

At the College of Information and Communications Technology (CICT), we embrace a holistic approach to education. Our belief extends beyond the confines of lecture halls and textbooks. It’s about fostering a dynamic ecosystem where students not only gain knowledge but also forge meaningful connections and develop practical skills. Our student organizations serve as vibrant hubs—each one a microcosm of passion, creativity, and growth. Whether you’re immersed in lines of code, sketching user interfaces, or troubleshooting complex tech challenges, there’s a welcoming space for you.

Course Exclusive

Alima Artists' Guild

Alima Artists' Guild is an exclusive artist community within the Bachelor of Science in Entertainment and Multimedia Computing (BSEMC) program. Our mission? To promote collaboration and learning among EMC students. Through workshops, seminars, exhibits, and team-building activities, Alima provides a nurturing space for creativity to flourish. Whether you’re a digital artist, animator, or sound designer, join us and let your imagination thrive!

Athenaeum Keepers

A Fresh Beginning for BLIS Enthusiasts The Athenaeum Keepers, a dynamic group of Bachelor of Library and Information Science (BLIS) students, emerges as the newest organization under CICT. Our mission extends beyond the pages of books—we aim to widen the reach of the BLIS program and empower future Library and Information Science (LIS) professionals. How? By enriching their skills, fostering competencies, and instilling a deep appreciation for libraries and information centers. As stewards of knowledge, we uphold integrity, creativity, and competence.

Chroma

What is Chroma? Chroma, an exclusive organization for students in the Bachelor of Science in Entertainment and Multimedia Computing (BSEMC) program, embodies creativity, innovation, and collaboration. As outlined in our Constitution and By-laws, Chroma serves as a guiding force for all EMC-related student endeavors. Our mission? To propel the progressive nature of the EMC discipline by undertaking impactful projects and nurturing essential requirements.
